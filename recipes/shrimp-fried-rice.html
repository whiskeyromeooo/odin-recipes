Shrimp Fried Rice

Description:

Shrimp Fried Rice is one of my go-to 30-minute meals and my family can’t get enough of it. Shrimp and rice loaded with veggies and egg is a protein-packed and easy dinner. Fried rice is the best way to use leftover cooked rice.

Ingredients:

  • 4 Cups of at least a day old rice (refrigerated works best)
  • 15-20 pcs raw Shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Mixed Vegetables (I use the frozen ones for convenience)
  • 2-3 Eggs, beaten
  • Green Onions chopped
  • Either Soy or Oyster Sauce (soy for authenticity, but personally I like oyster sauce better)
  • Sesame Oil (optional, but this makes the dish more authentic)
  • Garlic, half a head and chopped
  • White Onion, 1 bulb and chopped
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Canola Oil for cooking

Steps:

  1. Cook the shrimp on medium-high heat, about a minute per side, then set a side (be careful not to overcook or they might get rubbery)
  2. Pour eggs into the hot pan and scramble until it's no longer runny, set aside
  3. Sauté garlic, onions, and green onions (leave a bit for garnishing)
  4. After a few seconds, add in the first half of your rice (cooking the rice in batches makes the fried rice less oily)
  5. Add your mixed veggies, and the rest of your rice along with it
  6. Add 2-3 tbsps of oyster or soy sauce, keep stir-frying on high heat
  7. Add the cooked shrimp and eggs you set aside a while ago, and you can now serve it with a bit of green onions on top
